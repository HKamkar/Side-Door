# Anti-AI writing rules

The fastest way for a hiring manager to dismiss a resume is to recognize the AI tic. These rules exist to make the writing sound like the user wrote it, not like an LLM did.

## The 7-second scan test

A hiring manager spends roughly 7 seconds on a first-pass resume scan. In those 7 seconds, they should be able to read:
- A summary that names the type of role and what makes this candidate distinctive.
- The most relevant bullet of the most recent role.
- Two more bullets that mirror the JD's top requirements.

If any of those sentences would lose the scanner — too long, too generic, too padded — rewrite it.

## The read-out-loud test

After writing, read every sentence out loud. If a sentence takes more than ONE breath to read, it's too long. Cut it or split it.

Apply this rule mercilessly to the summary, the first bullet of each role, and any cover-letter-style flourish.

## Bullet formula

Every bullet follows: **[Action verb] + [what you did] + [how / at what scale] + [measurable result]**

- Action verb: specific (designed, shipped, owned, decided) — not generic (worked on, helped with, was involved in).
- What you did: the actual thing, not the abstraction.
- How / scale: team size, system size, scope, timeframe — gives the impact context.
- Measurable result: a number, a before/after, a stated outcome.

Examples:

- ❌ "Responsible for leading cross-functional teams to drive growth initiatives."
- ✅ "Led a 3-team initiative to rebuild onboarding; lifted activation 22% in Q2."

- ❌ "Leveraged data-driven insights to optimize the customer journey."
- ✅ "Ran 14 A/B tests on the signup flow; shipped the 4 that lifted conversion; killed the rest."

## Bullet count per role

- Current / most recent role: **5–7 bullets**.
- Previous roles (most recent 2): **3–5 bullets each**.
- Older roles (5+ years ago): **2–3 bullets**.
- Roles older than 10 years: **headline only**, unless directly relevant to the target role.

More than 7 bullets per role = the reader skims.

## Summary section rules

- 2–3 SHORT sentences.
- Each sentence under 20 words.
- No sentence lists more than 2 things.
- Lead with the most relevant credential (years + the specific kind of work) — not adjectives.

**Bad**: "Results-driven leader with a passion for growth and a track record of success in fast-paced environments."

**Good**: "Senior backend engineer with 8 years shipping payment-platform infrastructure at scale. Owned the migration that cut p99 from 800ms to 120ms at PrevCo. Now looking to take the same playbook into a regulated EU fintech."

## Patterns to kill

### Stacked adjectives
- ❌ "cross-functional, data-driven, customer-centric"
- ✅ Pick ONE, back it with a concrete example.

### Preamble clauses
- ❌ "Leveraging deep expertise in marketplace dynamics, led the team that..."
- ✅ "Led the team that..."

### Filler phrases
Cut any of these on sight:
- "demonstrating ability to"
- "showcasing expertise in"
- "with a track record of"
- "needed to drive"
- "spanning"
- "leveraging"
- "utilizing"
- "in order to"

### Compound noun piles
- ❌ "AI-driven product opportunity identification and execution"
- ✅ "Built an AI product from idea to production."

### Gerund tacks (the AI tic)
- ❌ "Shipped X, delivering Y while maintaining Z."
- ✅ Two sentences. "Shipped X. Maintained Z."

### "Led X, Y, and Z across N" piles
- ❌ "Led product, marketing, and engineering across 4 regions."
- ✅ One action per bullet. Split.

### Em dashes (em dash is the strongest AI tell)
- ❌ "Shipped feature X — increased revenue 12%."
- ✅ "Shipped feature X; increased revenue 12%." or "Shipped feature X. Revenue rose 12%."

This rule is a hard ban in resume bullets and the summary. The cover letter is more flexible but still: prefer commas, periods, colons, semicolons, or parentheses.

### Generic verb piles
- ❌ "Worked on, helped with, was involved in" — vague verbs hide low actual contribution.
- ✅ Specific verb. If the user can't pick one, the bullet might not be worth keeping.

## Action verb variety

Don't lead every bullet in the same role with "Led" or "Managed." Vary:

Design / Build / Ship / Own / Decide / Architect / Launch / Migrate / Refactor / Audit / Reduce / Grow / Land / Restart / Recover / Mentor / Hire / Promote / Wind down / Sunset

Match the verb to the actual action. Don't pick "Architect" for a tactical project just for variety.

## Quality checklist before output

- [ ] Summary references the specific role / industry / company stage.
- [ ] First 2 bullets of each role are the most relevant to this JD.
- [ ] Metrics in 60%+ of bullets across the resume.
- [ ] No em dashes.
- [ ] No phrase from the kill list above.
- [ ] No bullet over 2 lines.
- [ ] Action verbs varied (not all "Led" or "Managed").
- [ ] Level of language matches the role's seniority band.
- [ ] Every bullet traces to a specific fact from the resume or profile / stories bank.
- [ ] Business model, scope, and responsibilities match what the user actually stated.
- [ ] A hiring manager scanning for 7 seconds would see the fit.

If any box is unchecked, fix before showing the user.
