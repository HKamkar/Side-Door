# Level calibration

The same accomplishment reads differently depending on the seniority of the role you're targeting. Read the JD's level first, then emphasize accordingly.

## How to read the JD's level

Signals from the posting:
- **Title.** Engineer / Senior / Staff / Principal — or — IC / Manager / Director / VP / C-suite.
- **Years required.** 3+ = mid. 5+ = senior. 8+ = staff. 10+ = principal or above.
- **Scope language.** "Owns" vs. "drives" vs. "contributes" — ownership scales with level.
- **Reporting line.** "Reports to CTO" implies senior IC or higher; "reports to manager" implies mid.
- **Comp band.** If stated, calibrate against market for that level + region.

If the user's seniority is one band higher than the JD asks, that's a flag — overqualification reads as a flight risk. Address it in the cover letter.

## What to emphasize per band

### Executive (VP, C-suite)
- **Lead with**: strategy, P&L ownership, board-level communication, organizational design, hiring senior leaders, cross-functional alignment at the exec level.
- **De-emphasize**: tactical execution, individual contributor work, day-to-day operations.
- **Bullet shape**: business impact, not task completion. "Grew the segment from $X to $Y in 18 months by repositioning the product line and rebuilding the GTM motion" — not "Led marketing campaigns."

### Director
- **Lead with**: program ownership, team building (sizing, hiring, structure), cross-functional leadership, operational excellence, stakeholder management.
- **Balance**: strategic thinking with execution capability. Show both upward (exec alignment) and downward (team development) influence.
- **Bullet shape**: outcomes that span multiple quarters and multiple people. "Built and ran the X function from 0 to 12 reports, shipped 3 platform launches, lifted Y metric 40%."

### Senior IC / Tech Lead / Senior Manager (people-leading or scope-leading)
- **Lead with**: hands-on expertise, technical/functional depth, mentorship, direct impact on projects.
- **Balance**: deep work + influence-without-authority across teams.
- **Bullet shape**: concrete deliverables and project outcomes, with the user as the named driver. "Designed and shipped the X service; cut p99 from 800ms to 120ms; mentored 3 ICs who later led related projects."

### Mid-level IC
- **Lead with**: skills used, projects delivered, problems solved.
- **De-emphasize**: scope claims they don't have ("led" should mean led, not "was on the team that").
- **Bullet shape**: clear cause-and-effect. "Built X using Y; reduced Z by N%."

### Junior IC
- **Lead with**: technologies + projects + measurable improvements. Internship/student work fine if recent.
- **Bullet shape**: short, specific, learning-oriented OK if the role is junior.

## The honesty rule

If the user's actual scope was senior-IC and you're applying to a Director role, do NOT inflate. Use the cover letter to argue the case for a step up. Inflating the resume is the fastest way to get caught in the interview.

If the user's actual scope was Director and you're applying to a Senior IC role, the issue is OVER-qualification — strip the management bullets and lead with the hands-on work the user did before management.

## Sanity check before writing

Pick one sentence: "This person is being hired as a [LEVEL] to [DO WHAT]." If you can't write that sentence honestly using only what's on the resume + profile, the tailoring is going to limp.
