# Banned phrases

Phrases that mark a cover letter or resume as generic or AI-written. Do not use these.

## Cover-letter clichés
- "I am writing to express my interest"
- "I am writing to apply for"
- "I would like to apply for"
- "I came across your job posting"
- "I was excited to see"
- "Please find attached"
- "I believe I would be a great fit"
- "I am a perfect match for this role"
- "I bring a wealth of experience"
- "passionate about" — replace with what the passion actually is
- "results-driven"
- "proven track record"
- "go-getter", "self-starter", "team player", "rockstar", "ninja", "guru" — never
- "thinking outside the box"
- "synergy"
- "leverage" as a verb when "use" would do
- "delve into"

## AI-tic phrases
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "in the ever-evolving landscape"
- "at the intersection of"
- "I am eager to contribute"
- "I would relish the opportunity"
- "I am excited about the opportunity"
- "I am thrilled to apply"
- "navigate the complexities of"
- "drive impactful results"
- "deliver value"
- "spearhead transformative initiatives"
- "aligns perfectly with"
- "aligns seamlessly with"
- "living and breathing {anything}"
- "in line with what I observe"
- "adopting {processes / methodologies / frameworks}"

## Punctuation bans (cover letters and resume bullets)

- **Em dash (`—`)** and **en dash (`–`)** — strongest AI tells. Replace with comma, period, colon, semicolon, parentheses, or hyphen (`-`). Hyphens are fine; dashes are not.
- Curly quotes ("smart quotes") in plain-text drafts — fine in DOCX since Word handles them, but flag if pasted into a plain-text application form.

## Hollow openings
- "Dear Sir/Madam" — use "Dear Hiring Team at {Company}" instead
- "To Whom It May Concern"
- "I hope this email finds you well"

## Resume bullet anti-patterns
- "Responsible for" — start with a verb instead
- "Helped with" — use the actual verb (designed, built, owned, shipped, debugged)
- "Worked on X" — replace with what was done and the outcome
- "Various" / "many" / "multiple" without a number — be concrete or drop the word
- "Wearing many hats"
- "Cross-functional collaboration" — name the function and what was collaborated on

If a phrase here is genuinely the most accurate description in context, surface that to the user and let them decide. Default is to rewrite.
